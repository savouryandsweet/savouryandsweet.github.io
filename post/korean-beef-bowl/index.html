Korean beef bowl

Hannah ,  November 17, 2020

Hi friends!

Are you missing your favourite Korean restaurant? I know I am! This Korean bibimbap bowl has been a goto of ours since we got it from a meal delivery box a few months ago. It is definitely not a traditional type of bibimbap but it’s quick and easy for weeknights.

Ingredients:

  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into rounds
  • 1 zucchini, cut into rounds
  • 1/2 lb mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp sugar (white, brown or honey works here)
  • Sriracha, to taste
  • 2 cups cooked rice, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Pan fry the vegetables in a little olive oil, then remove and set aside. Add carrot first since it cooks the slowest.
  2. Cook the ground beef with the whites of the green onions add 1 tbsp of soy sauce towards the end of cooking.
  3. Mix up your sauce: 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp sesame oil and 1 tsp sugar.
  4. Divide rice among 4 bowls and top with meat, vegetables, sauce and green part of green onions. Drizzle with sriracha if you like things spicy. Top with fried egg if you want extra protein.

Look, this Korean beef bowl isn’t the most beautiful meal I’ve made, but when a meal is this easy to make and the whole family loves it then you know I have to share it! If you’re vegan or vegetarian, this meal can be made with TVP or your favourite ground beef substitute.
