Faux meat: is it the real deal?!

Hannah ,  October 7, 2020 â€¢ 10 min read

Hi friends!

Have you tried the new faux meat products available these days? Everyone from Target to A&W are getting on the bandwagon. Is it the new plant based burger that will satisfy even the beef lovers among us? Let’s find out!

I first tried an impossible burger at a cute little bistro in Los Altos and it was definitely delicious. I do like all burgers though, from salmon to beef to black bean, so I’m not the most picky.

I have also seen both impossible meat and beyond burger meat available at many grocery stores like Target, Costco and Safeway.

Now let’s look at the nutrient breakdown for 4oz portions:

Impossible:

240 calories, 14g fat, 8g saturated fat, 19g protein, 370mg sodium

Beyond meat:

250 calories, 18g fat, 6g saturated fat, 20g protein, 390mg sodium

85% lean beef:

240 calories, 21g fat, 6g saturated fat, 19g protein, 80mg sodium

At first glance, it is obvious that the faux meats are MUCH higher in sodium. So, if you are on a low sodium diet for chronic kidney disease, chronic heart disease or any other reason then the beef burger may be your better option.

The next thing that pops out is the similarity between calories, protein and saturated fats! Of course, the beef burger has 7g more total fat than the impossible burger. A diet high in saturated fat is linked to increased rates of heart disease, but there are of course other factors like physical activity and smoking that will play a role as well.

In terms of sustainability, the faux meat options are going to require much less water to produce and will produce less greenhouse gases. Yay! But before deciding to include these new burger options, it is important to note that these are HIGHLY processed and do not contain as much of the flavonoids as eating whole plants.

In conclusion, these new burgers are a fun way to include plant based meals, especially if you have a carnivore who doesn’t generally accept other faux meats. However, I would recommend choosing these less often and trying your hand at making your own plant based burger using whole grains, bean, lentils and veggies! check out the link below for my favourite one!
